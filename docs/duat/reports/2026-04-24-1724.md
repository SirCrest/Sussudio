## Gaps Identified (Gate 6)
- Security and trust boundaries for automation pipe ACL fallback, token sourcing, local malicious clients, and destructive commands were not reviewed as a security model.
- A/V sync, timestamp correctness, HDR metadata, frame accounting, and color-space correctness need dedicated media invariants.
- Native resource lifetime across D3D, Media Foundation, CUDA/NVDEC, COM, WASAPI, XU handles, MJPEG, and NVML needs soak-proof auditing.
- Crash recovery and startup repair for interrupted recordings, exports, stale settings, and abandoned temp sessions were only indirectly covered.
- Settings/schema migration needs versioning and repair coverage for missing, malformed, out-of-range, and non-finite persisted fields.
- Hardware control safety needs rollback, readback, timeout, and multi-process arbitration review.
- Confirmation/busy-state policy is inconsistent for destructive flashback purge, export overwrite, close while recording, and blocked restart flows.
- Thread-affinity beyond `AudioPeak` needs review for callback paths that write UI-observed state.
- Automation needs a command matrix distinguishing requested, applied, and snapshot-verified responses.
- Sleep/hotplug recovery needs concrete tests during active recording, flashback buffering, and export.
- Thin direct coverage remains for core capture/recording/flashback internals including `MfSourceReaderVideoCapture`, `UnifiedVideoCapture`, `LibAvRecordingSink`, `LibAvEncoder`, `RecordingArtifactManager`, `FlashbackEncoderSink`, `FlashbackPlaybackController`, `FlashbackDecoder`, `D3D11PreviewRenderer.Rendering`, `NativeXuAtCommandProvider`, `RuntimePaths`, and HDR validation.
